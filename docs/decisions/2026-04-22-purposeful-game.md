# 2026-04-22 — Purposeful game, not passive sandbox

**Status:** Accepted

**Context:** Initial design from Waverly's voice memos read as a sandbox (wander, pop balloons, watch Fruity). Risk: kids lose interest in sandboxes without a hook. Also wanted clear micro and macro goals for her to feel progress.

**Decision:** Add tap-to-move / double-tap-to-shoot-magic controls, a magic meter, rain cloud obstacles, a world-painting progress system, and a celebration sequence on completion. Keep all of Waverly's design language (Fruity the unicorn, Firey the dragon, rainbow farts, glitter painting the world).

**Alternatives considered:**
- Stay sandbox — risk of low engagement
- Add scoring or lives — felt punitive for a young kid

**Consequences:** Sessions have a shape (micro goal: paint this area; macro goal: paint the world). The weird details from her interviews stay in. The "won't win design awards but Waverly loves it" line is the success metric.
